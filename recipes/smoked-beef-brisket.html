Smoked Beef Brisket - My simplified way

Smoking foods can be a really daunting task. It's really no big deal as long as you get the right smoker that fits your needs. I personally don't really want to fiddle with smoking food on a charcoal grill, so I use an electric upright smoker. After it smokes for around 2-3 hours, the brisket is placed in the oven at a low temperature for around 9 hours. Don't be afraid of the time, it'll be fine. You'll only have the most moist cut of meat you've ever eaten in your life.

What you'll need:

  • 15 lb moist cut beef brisket
  • Brown Sugar
  • Chili Powder
  • Smoked Paprika
  • Kosher Salt
  • Ground Black Pepper
  • Whatever spice you feel like using
  • Tin Foil
  • Hickory Wood Chips

Directions

  1. Preheat your smoker to about 225 degrees fahrenheit.
  2. Rinse the brisket in cold water until the water that runs off of it is clear. Pat dry with heavy paper towels.
  3. Score the fat cap on the top of the brisket. About 1 inch apart for each score.
  4. Create your rub and get creative! I didn't put amounts because I never measured. You will need a lot more salt than the rest of your spices, since it's a large cut of meat. You will NOT go wrong!
  5. Prepare your smoker and wood chips as per manual's directions.
  6. Place the beef on a rack in the smoker and smoke for abotu 2-3 hours or until the beef hits around 140-150 degrees fahrenheit.

    7. Finishing in the oven

    1. Preheat your oven to 225 degrees fahrenheit.
    2. Completely wrap the smoked brisket with tin foil with the fat cap and foil seam facing up.
    3. Transfer the brisket to the preheated oven for about 8-9 hours. I said this was simplified, not shorter.
    4. When meat is finished, let it rest for about 45 minutes before slicing. It'll be fall apart tender! Serve with BBQ sauce or just eat it plain. Enjoy!
