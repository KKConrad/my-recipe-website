Coconut Butterscotch Oatmeal Cookies

These oatmeal cookies are dressed up for any occasion. It can be served at parties, given as gifts or lunch box desserts. They are rolled in powdered sugar before baking which decorates the cookie with beautifully cracked tops. This recipe also features two of my all-time favorite ingredients: Coconut and Butterscotch!

What you'll need:

  • 14 tablespoons (1 3/4 sticks) unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 3/4 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 2/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/4 cups old-fashioned oats
  • 1 large egg
  • 3/4 c dried blueberries
  • 1 1/4 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 1/4 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 cup sweetened shredded coconut
  • 1/2 cup butterscotch chips
  • 1 cup confectioners’ sugar

Directions

  1. Heat the oven to 375°F.
  2. Combine the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment on high and cream together until light and fluffy, about 3 minutes.
  3. Add the eggs and vanilla and mix until incorporated, about 1 minute.
  4. Add the flour, oats, cinnamon, salt, baking soda, and coconut, if using, and mix until just combined, about 30 seconds.
  5. Put the confectioners’ sugar in a small bowl. Scoop and roll the dough between your palms into golf-ball-sized balls. Toss in the bowl of confectioners’ sugar until completely covered and arrange the cookies 2 to 3 inches apart on a greased or parchment-lined baking sheet.
  6. Bake the cookies for 9 to 10 minutes, until golden brown and crackled.
